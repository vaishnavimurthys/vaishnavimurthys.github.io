Student Satisfaction using Data Mining

Designed and administered a targeted survey on student satisfaction, gathering over 500 data points to ensure data sufficiency and reliability.

Performed Exploratory data analysis(EDA) on student feedback data, focusing on attributes like recommendation likeliness, course clarity, teaching quality, and extracurricular activities, to find what affected student satisfaction.

Developed multiple machine learning models (Decision Trees, Random Forest, SVM, and Logistic Regression) to analyze and predict student satisfaction levels, with Decision Trees and SVM providing the highest accuracy and actionable insights.

Employed data visualization techniques in PowerBI to create interactive reports, effectively communicating actionable recommendations to university stakeholders.

Prioritized improvements in course clarity, teaching quality, and extracurricular activities while identifying areas for development in communication availability, library accessibility, academic advising, and accommodation to enhance overall student satisfaction.
